## Overview

A small laser-engraved wooden lantern themed for Halloween, modeled in Fusion 360 and assembled from pre-cut wood. The intent was to combine a graphic engraving with a functional enclosure that houses a light, wrapping several skills into one seasonal object.

![Assembled Halloween lantern with engraved ghost design](/assets/projects/halloween-laser-cut-wooden-lantern/screenshot-2023-10-26-9.23.30-am.png)

## The Problem

This was one of the first class projects, meant to pull together the skills we had learned so far — Fusion 360, graphic design, and laser cutting — into a single built object. The goal was a lantern that looked the part while still giving easy access to the light inside.

## Design & Development

I started by finding a ghost design online and laser engraving it onto the front piece of wood. The front with the engraved design, the back, the bottom, and the sides were all joined together with nails and glue. The top, however, was not glued — I used velcro so the mini electric candle inside could be swapped in and out easily. Because the wood pieces were pre-cut to dimension, the main tools were the laser engraver and the nail gun, which was my first time using one.

## Outcome & Reflection

This project was the first to bring together everything we had learned — Fusion 360, graphic design, and laser cutting — and it taught me how to use a nail gun along the way. Overall I had a fun time making it and got hands-on practice applying those skills to a real object.
